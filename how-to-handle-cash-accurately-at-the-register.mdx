## Overview

Accuracy at the register is more than avoiding mistakes — it is about building trust. Every dollar you touch represents responsibility to both the customer and the store. Consistent cash handling positions every associate as dependable, professional, and trustworthy.

<Callout kind="tip">

Cashiers who double-check large bills reduce drawer shortages by up to 40%. Accuracy is not just a habit — it is measurable profit protection.

</Callout>

## Stay Calm Under Pressure

When the line is long and customers are impatient, errors sneak in. The best associates know that accuracy beats speed every time. Slowing down just enough to double-check bills and coins proves to the customer — and to yourself — that you are in control even when things are hectic.

## Take Control of Every Transaction

A smooth, error-free transaction creates customer confidence. Build these habits on every transaction:

- Count out loud when making change
- Verify bills carefully before accepting
- Never let distractions pull your attention from the drawer
- Keep the customer's bill on the counter until the transaction is complete
- Close the drawer fully after every transaction

These small habits make the difference between an average cashier and one customers trust to get it right every time.

## Accuracy Pays Off

The numbers make the case:

- Cashiers who double-check large bills reduce drawer shortages by up to 40%
- Stores that train associates in accuracy-first habits see fewer customer disputes
- Faster end-of-shift balancing reduces manager time and stress
- Clean cash handling creates traceability that protects honest employees

## Common Causes of Cash Errors

- Rushing during peak hours
- Accepting a bill without checking its denomination
- Counting change mentally instead of out loud
- Leaving the drawer open between transactions
- Getting distracted by a customer conversation mid-count

<Callout kind="alert">

Never count change in your head during a busy shift. Always count out loud and place bills on the counter before closing the drawer. One rushed transaction can cause a shortage that takes an entire shift to investigate.

</Callout>

## Key Principle

Handling cash accurately at the register is not just a task — it is your reputation. When you treat every dollar like it matters, you earn the trust of your customers, your coworkers, and your managers. Accuracy is the foundation of professionalism at every level of store operations.
