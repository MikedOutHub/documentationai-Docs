## Behavioral Signs

Watch for customers who display these behaviors:

<ul>
  <li>Immediately heads toward security cameras or EAS tag areas upon entering</li>
  <li>Lingers near the point of sale without making a purchase</li>
  <li>Handles many items while watching store personnel</li>
  <li>Frequently glances toward exits</li>
  <li>Avoids eye contact with staff while lingering in certain areas</li>
  <li>Nervously checks surroundings before moving to another area</li>
  <li>Focused on items without checking price tags</li>
  <li>Frequently checks exits or hides items in bags or clothing</li>
</ul>

Shoplifters do not always fit a specific profile. Train staff to observe behavior, not appearance.

### Who shoplifts

Shoplifters may act alone or in groups. Underage shoplifters are more common than many operators expect. Never make assumptions based on appearance — observe behavior only.

## Physical Signs

### Physical Signs to Watch For

<ul>
  <li>Wearing oversized or excessive clothing that could conceal items</li>
  <li>Carrying large bags, strollers, or oversized purses</li>
  <li>Working in pairs or groups where one person distracts staff</li>
</ul>

## Prevention & Deterrence

### How to Deter Shoplifters

<Callout kind="tip">
  Engage customers proactively — A simple greeting acknowledges their presence and signals that staff are attentive
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Engage customers proactively">
    Engage customers proactively — A simple greeting acknowledges their presence and signals that staff are attentive
  </Step>
  <Step title="Place high-value items near the checkout">
    Place high-value items near the checkout — Reduces opportunity for concealment in low-traffic areas
  </Step>
  <Step title="Monitor all store exits">
    Monitor all store exits — Ensure exits are visible to staff at all times
  </Step>
  <Step title="Install cameras at key locations">
    Install cameras at entrances, exits, and high-value displays — Serves as a visual deterrent and provides evidence
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use electronic article surveillance systems">
    Use electronic article surveillance (EAS) systems — Deters theft and alerts staff to unauthorized exits
  </Step>
  <Step title="Train staff regularly">
    Train staff regularly — Employees should know what to look for and how to respond without confrontation
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Store Layout as a Prevention Tool

Store Layout as a Prevention Tool  
Thoughtful store design reduces shoplifting opportunity:

<ul>
  <li>Keep high-value items in high-visibility locations</li>
  <li>Eliminate blind spots with mirrors or camera placement</li>
  <li>Position staff near areas with the highest theft risk</li>
  <li>Ensure checkout counter has clear sightlines across the store</li>
</ul>

### Important: How Staff Should Respond

<Callout kind="danger">
  Staff should never physically confront or detain a suspected shoplifter.
</Callout>

Important: How Staff Should Respond  
Staff should never physically confront or detain a suspected shoplifter. The correct response is to:

<ul>
  <li>Acknowledge the customer with a greeting</li>
  <li>Offer assistance — this signals awareness</li>
  <li>Alert a manager if behavior continues</li>
  <li>Document and report — do not pursue</li>
</ul>
